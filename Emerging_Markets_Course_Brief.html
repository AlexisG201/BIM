Course Brief · Year 1 · International Management

Emerging Markets
Analysis

A semester-long tour of ASEAN and South Asia — what makes these markets "emerging," how they compare to Taiwan, and what that means for the businesses operating in them.

Tuesdays · 09:00–12:00 18 weeks Groups of 3 for major assignments Asst. Prof. Alexis Goh · Office MA309

Why this course

Markets don't emerge in a vacuum

Globalization and a hyper-connected internet economy are accelerating growth across many countries — but "growth" doesn't automatically mean "opportunity." This course builds a working knowledge of major ASEAN and South Asian economies and uses Taiwan as a constant point of comparison throughout.

Every country week is built around the same idea: learn the basics, compare them to something you already know (Taiwan), and then use what you've learned to form a position on a real business question. There usually isn't a single "correct" answer — what matters is whether your reasoning holds up.

  • 01Know the geography, demographics, politics, and economic profile of major ASEAN and South Asian markets.
  • 02Understand why these countries are labelled "emerging markets" — and where that label breaks down.
  • 03Compare each market to Taiwan across economy, cost of living, risk, and trade.
  • 04Build and defend a reasoned business judgment — even when there's no single right answer.

The semester at a glance

The Market Route

Eighteen weeks, stop by stop. Slides for each stop go up on TronClass before class — read ahead if you want a head start, but the in-class session is where the real discussion happens.

Setting Out
WEEK 01

Course Introduction & Defining "Emerging Markets"

What actually makes a market "emerging"? We set the definitions, look at where Taiwan fits, and map out the semester ahead.

ASEAN Market Day
WEEK 02

Indonesia

The world's largest archipelago and the 4th most populous country on Earth. We size it up against Taiwan and look at Taiwan's New Southbound Policy.

WEEK 03

Malaysia

A multi-ethnic, resource-rich economy with a real stake in global electronics manufacturing — much like Taiwan. Competitor, or partner?

WEEK 04

Thailand

One of the original "Tiger Cub" economies. Cost of living, credit ratings, and the "Thailand 4.0" growth strategy.

WEEK 05

Individual Assignment I Due

No class this week. Submit via TronClass — see Assignments below.

WEEK 06

Singapore & Brunei

Two very different paths to wealth in the same region — a global financial hub, and an oil-rich sultanate.

WEEK 07

Vietnam

Manufacturing's rising star, and a major destination for Taiwanese investment. What does "the next China" actually mean?

WEEK 08

The Philippines

A young, English-speaking population and a services-driven economy — a different model from Taiwan's manufacturing base.

WEEK 09

Mid-Semester Writing Task

In-class · pen-and-paper · up to 3 hours. AI research tools allowed; final answers handwritten. Two questions — see Assignments.

WEEK 10

ASEAN in General

Zooming out: what ASEAN actually is, how it works as a bloc, and where Taiwan fits — or doesn't.

WEEK 11

Myanmar & Cambodia

Two markets most investors approach cautiously — plus, Group Assignment II briefing.

South Asia Market Day
WEEK 12

India

1.4 billion people, 28 states, and a fast-growing relationship with Taiwan's semiconductor industry.

WEEK 13

Pakistan & Bangladesh

Similar wages, very different investment stories. We dig into why.

WEEK 14

Sri Lanka & Emerging Market Risk

A case study in what "emerging market risk" looks like when it materializes — the 2022 economic collapse.

Final Stretch
WEEK 15

Wildcard Week

Class vote: one more country or region, chosen by you.

WEEK 16

Group Assignment II Due

Submission due. Final presentation country list and details given out.

WEEK 17–18

Final Group Presentations

Each group introduces their adopted country to the class.

The work

Assignments

Individual Assignment I

10%
Due Week 5 · TronClass

Choose ONE emerging market country NOT covered in this course. Using at least three sources, argue: is this country's growth driven mainly by its people, its resources, or its institutions?

600–800 words. AI tools may help with research, but the final position and writing must be your own.

Mid-Semester Writing Task

15%
Week 9 · In-class

Two questions, pen-and-paper, up to 3 hours. One asks for a market recommendation; the other asks you to argue which ASEAN market is most "overrated."

AI tools may be used during the session for research and structuring — but every final answer must be handwritten.

Group Assignment II

15%
Due Week 16 · Groups of 3

Your group advises a Taiwanese SME entering ONE South Asian market covered in class. Write a 1,000–1,200 word market entry brief: why this country, why now, the biggest risk, and a 90-day action plan.

Briefed in Week 11.

Final Group Presentation

15%
Weeks 17–18 · Groups of 3

Your group "adopts" a country from a list given out in Week 16 — one we haven't covered in class. Introduce it to potential Taiwanese investors: where it is, what its economy runs on, the opportunity, the risk, and your recommendation.

10–12 minutes per group, plus 3 minutes of Q&A.

Heads up: bring your phone to class, but expect it to spend quiz time face-down at the front of the room. Pen-and-paper quizzes run during the first 15 minutes of most weeks.
