Phase 1 Foundations of Decision-Making Weeks 1–4
Week 1 What Is a Good Decision? Process vs. outcome ▼
Topics
  • Decision vs. choice: process matters as much as outcome
  • Outcome bias: a good decision can turn out badly — and vice versa
  • Why this distinction matters for accountability
  • Intro: AI as a decision supporter, not a decision-maker
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.PollDid your last major decision turn out well because of good judgment or good luck? Anonymous class poll and discussion.
  • 2.DiscussionTwo scenarios: a manager who followed data and failed; one who ignored data and succeeded. Was either decision good?
  • 3.Case7-Eleven Taiwan adds a new product across 25,000+ stores. What makes this a good or bad decision regardless of outcome?
  • 4.GroupCategorize 3 witnessed decisions across the 2×2 matrix: process quality vs. outcome quality.
  • 5.ReflectExit ticket: In 3 sentences, what does responsible decision-making mean to you? Saved for Week 18 comparison.
Week 2 Cognitive Biases & Mental Shortcuts System 1 vs. System 2 ▼
Topics
  • Kahneman's System 1 (fast, intuitive) vs. System 2 (slow, deliberate)
  • Key biases: anchoring, confirmation bias, availability heuristic, groupthink
  • Parrish's four defaults: emotional, ego, social, inertia
  • How AI outputs can become a new anchoring point
  • Taiwan context: herd investing behavior, crypto bubbles
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.QuizClassic anchoring exercise: two groups see different numbers, then estimate the same quantity. The gap is always surprising.
  • 2.DiscussionHave you ever changed your answer after seeing ChatGPT's response — even when you weren't sure it was right?
  • 3.CaseTaiwanese retail investors during the 2021 crypto surge. Which biases explain the herd behavior?
  • 4.GroupGiven a Taiwan business scenario, identify the 2 biases most likely to appear.
  • 5.Debate"AI-generated recommendations reduce cognitive bias." Half for, half against. 3 minutes each side.
Week 3 Ethical Frameworks as Decision Lenses Utilitarianism · Deontology · Virtue ethics · ESG ▼
Topics
  • Utilitarianism — Who benefits most overall?
  • Deontology — What rule or duty applies?
  • Virtue ethics — What would a person of good character do?
  • ESG/CSR as applied ethics: how each framework maps onto corporate sustainability metrics
  • Applying multiple lenses — and ESG criteria — to the same decision
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.DiscussionA Taiwanese food company discovers a quality issue one week before launch. What does each framework recommend?
  • 2.GroupFramework Sorting: apply all 3 frameworks to different short cases. Do they agree?
  • 3.Role PlayBoard members of a fictional Taiwanese tech company, each assigned a different framework, debate launching an AI customer scoring system.
  • 4.V3 CaseTSMC's water usage during Taiwan's 2021 drought. Apply all three frameworks and connect to TSMC's ESG disclosures.
  • 5.ReflectWhich framework feels most natural to you — and which is hardest to apply? Why?
Week 4 Organizational & Cultural Decision-Making Face, hierarchy, guanxi ▼
Topics
  • How organizations actually decide: committees, hierarchy, consensus culture
  • Taiwan-specific dynamics: guanxi, face, deference to seniority
  • Why "yes" doesn't always mean yes in high-context cultures
  • Confucian values vs. transparent accountability
  • High-context (Taiwan) vs. low-context (Germany, US) decision styles
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.DiscussionHave you ever disagreed with a group decision but said nothing? Anonymous poll first, then open discussion.
  • 2.SimulationTeam A reaches consensus with no open disagreement; Team B uses structured debate. Same problem — compare results and how each felt.
  • 3.CaseA Taiwanese subsidiary receives instructions from HQ that conflict with local norms. Where does real decision authority sit?
  • 4.GroupDecision Mapping: draw the real vs. official decision flow in a Taiwanese organization you have worked at.
  • 5.DiscussionCan AI reduce face-saving pressures in group decisions through anonymous voting? What risks does that introduce?
Phase 2 AI as Decision Partner Weeks 5–8
Week 5 AI Basics for Managers What AI actually does ▼
Topics
  • What AI actually does: pattern recognition from data, not reasoning
  • Key types (business focus): classification, prediction, recommendation, generative AI
  • What "training data" means and why it matters for decisions
  • The black box problem and why explainability matters
  • Taiwan context: TAIDE (Trustworthy AI Dialogue Engine), sovereign AI
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.PollScale 1–5: how well do you understand how ChatGPT works? Poll, explain the mechanism, re-poll after.
  • 2.DiscussionIf an AI recommends a job candidate and you hire them — who made the decision?
  • 3.DemoLive demo: same prompt with slightly different phrasing produces different outputs. What does this reveal?
  • 4.GroupAI or Not?: given 6 Taiwanese business decisions, guess which ones used AI and explain your reasoning.
  • 5.CaseTaiwan's TAIDE project: why build a local AI model instead of using GPT? What values does a "sovereign AI" embed?
Week 6 AI as Decision Support — The Upside Augmentation vs. replacement ▼
Topics
  • Where AI genuinely helps: speed, pattern detection, consistency
  • Taiwan examples: fraud detection (LINE Pay, EasyCard), credit scoring, logistics (Momo, PChome)
  • Medical triage (NHI data), predictive maintenance (TSMC supply chain)
  • Decision augmentation vs. decision replacement — the key distinction
  • Industry 4.0 in Taiwan: manufacturing AI adoption rates
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.DiscussionWhat decisions does an algorithm already make for you — Foodpanda routing, Spotify, YouTube? Are you OK with this?
  • 2.CaseTaiwanese logistics company uses AI routing; on-time delivery up 18%. What decisions should remain with the human driver?
  • 3.GroupAI Decision Audit: for a given Taiwanese industry, map where AI could augment 3 specific decisions. Identify benefit and risk for each.
  • 4.Debate"A bank's AI system should be able to reject a loan application without human review." Structured debate with assigned sides.
  • 5.ReflectOne decision in your intended career where you'd welcome AI help; one where you'd resist it. Explain both.
Week 7 Human-AI Collaboration — Trust & Override When to trust, when to override ▼
Topics
  • When to trust AI output vs. when to override it
  • Over-reliance: automation bias and its consequences
  • Under-reliance: ignoring useful AI signals due to distrust or ego
  • Mollick's framework: AI as collaborator, not oracle
  • Psychological safety in questioning AI recommendations
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.SimulationAI hiring tool recommends rejecting a candidate; manager disagrees. Play out the deliberation: who overrides, how, and with what documentation?
  • 2.DiscussionA doctor disagrees with an AI diagnostic tool that is correct 91% of the time. Does she override it?
  • 3.V3 Live AILive AI Prompt & Critique (20 min): use ChatGPT or TAIDE on a Taiwanese business scenario, then audit the output in pairs for assumptions, cultural blind spots, and overconfidence.
  • 4.GroupTrust Calibration: for 5 AI recommendations, set the appropriate oversight level — full trust / verify then act / one input among many / discard.
  • 5.DiscussionYour manager tells you to implement an AI recommendation you disagree with. What do you do? Role-play escalation options.
Week 8 AI Bias, Fairness & Discrimination How AI inherits and amplifies bias ▼
Topics
  • How AI inherits and amplifies bias from training data
  • Types of bias: data bias, feedback loops, proxy discrimination
  • Taiwan angle: demographic proxies (district, age, gender) in credit and hiring algorithms
  • What does "fairness" mean? Individual vs. group fairness; cultural differences
  • Real cases: facial recognition errors, biased resume screening tools
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.CaseAmazon's AI hiring tool systematically downgraded resumes correlated with women. Could this happen at a Taiwanese tech company?
  • 2.DiscussionIf an AI loan system has higher rejection rates for applicants from certain districts in Taiwan — is that discrimination?
  • 3.GroupDesign a fairness checklist for a specific AI use case. What data should never be used? What audits are required?
  • 4.Role PlayA Taiwanese company's AI performance review system has been flagged for gender bias. Students play the employee, HR manager, AI vendor, and CEO.
  • 5.ReflectHave you ever felt an algorithm treated you unfairly? What made it feel unfair?

Phase 4 Applied Decision-Making Weeks 14–17
Week 14 Cross-Cultural Decision-Making High-context vs. low-context environments ▼
Topics
  • How culture shapes what counts as ethical and effective
  • High-context (Taiwan, Japan) vs. low-context (Germany, US) decision styles
  • Confucian values: harmony, face, loyalty, hierarchy — in tension with transparency
  • When global companies impose Western frameworks on Asian subsidiaries
  • AI systems trained on Western data: cultural blind spots for Taiwanese users
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.DiscussionA US HQ sends an AI-generated performance review policy to its Taiwan subsidiary — employees ranked and giving visible peer feedback. What cultural problems arise?
  • 2.CaseA Taiwanese manager must tell a senior colleague their project failed due to poor judgment. How does face and hierarchy affect whether and how this is communicated?
  • 3.Role PlayInternational negotiation: a Taiwanese company and a German partner disagree on a supply contract term. Play out the negotiation using each culture's natural decision style.
  • 4.GroupAI Cultural Audit: identify 3 types of decisions where Western-trained AI produces culturally inappropriate recommendations for Taiwanese users.
  • 5.ReflectDescribe a moment where cultural expectations conflicted with your own judgment about the right decision. What did you do?
Week 15 Crisis & High-Stakes Decision-Making Speed vs. deliberation ▼
Topics
  • The tension between speed and deliberation under pressure
  • How cognitive biases intensify in crisis situations
  • Crisis communication and decision transparency
  • Taiwan cases: food safety scandals, corporate governance failures
  • AI in crisis detection — and the risk of false alarms or missed signals
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.SimulationCrisis simulation: groups receive a breaking scenario (product contamination, cyberattack, regulatory investigation). 10 minutes to make and communicate 3 decisions. Which biases appeared?
  • 2.CaseThe 2014 gutter oil scandal: trace decision-making at each level — supplier, processor, distributor, food company, regulator. Where did the chain break?
  • 3.DiscussionDuring COVID, Taiwan's CDC made real-time decisions under extreme uncertainty. What made some of those decisions responsible even when outcomes were uncertain?
  • 4.GroupSpeed vs. Deliberation Audit: for 4 crisis types, how fast must a decision be made? What must not be skipped even under time pressure?
  • 5.Debate"Companies should have AI-generated crisis decision playbooks that managers are required to follow under certain trigger conditions."
Week 16 Automation, Jobs & Workforce Ethics The ethics of displacement ▼
Topics
  • The ethics of replacing workers with AI and automation
  • Taiwan manufacturing context: Industry 4.0, TSMC-adjacent supply chain automation
  • What obligations does a company have to workers it displaces?
  • AI in performance management: monitoring, scoring, and terminating by algorithm
  • Responsible transition: reskilling, communication, and dignity
Activities & Discussions
  • 1.DiscussionA Taiwanese manufacturer automates 200 jobs and uses the savings to hire 20 AI engineers. Is this ethical? Who should answer that question?
  • 2.CaseA logistics company's AI routing turns delivery riders into algorithm-followers. How does this change their autonomy? Is this deskilling?

  • 4.GroupDesign a responsible automation policy: how workers are informed, what support is offered, and what decisions the company will NOT automate.
