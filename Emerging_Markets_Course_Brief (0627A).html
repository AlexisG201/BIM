Tuesdays · 09:00–12:00 18 weeks Groups of 3 Asst. Prof. Alexis Goh · MA309

What this course is about

Taiwan doesn't exist in isolation

Taiwan depends on trade routes through the Middle East, manufacturing partnerships across ASEAN, energy suppliers in the Gulf, and an increasingly contested position in global supply chains. This course builds a working knowledge of the markets that matter most to Taiwan — and asks you to think critically about what those markets mean for business.

Every week follows the same pattern: learn the basics of a country, compare it to Taiwan, and discuss a real business question. The questions don't have single correct answers — what matters is whether your reasoning holds up.

Not all countries covered are formally classified as "emerging markets." They've been selected because of their strategic relevance to Taiwan's economy and business interests.

  • 01Know the geography, demographics, politics, and economy of major markets across ASEAN, South Asia, the Middle East, and Africa.
  • 02Understand the "emerging market" label — and where it breaks down.
  • 03Compare each market to Taiwan across economy, risk, and trade.
  • 04Build and defend a reasoned business judgment.
  • 05Use AI tools critically — as a research aid, not a substitute for thinking.

Good to know

How this course works

📱

Phones at the front during quizzes

Quizzes are pen-and-paper, first 15 minutes of class. Your phone goes face-down at the front of the room. Show up on time.

🤖

AI is allowed — think first

AI tools are permitted for research and writing in most assessments. But the position and reasoning must always be your own. AI can help you research; it can't think for you.

✍️

English is the language of instruction

This is an EMI course. All assessments are expected in English. Chinese responses in the mid-semester task are accepted but carry a score ceiling.

📂

Slides go up on TronClass before class

Layers 1 and 2 of each week are available before the session. The in-class discussion question is only revealed on the day — read ahead, but come ready to think.

🌏

Taiwan is the constant reference point

Every country is compared to Taiwan — size, wages, cost of living, credit ratings, trade relationships. You'll leave knowing where Taiwan sits in the world.

💬

There are no "correct" opinions

Most discussion questions and assignments are graded on the quality of your reasoning, not on reaching a particular conclusion. Think clearly, argue coherently.
