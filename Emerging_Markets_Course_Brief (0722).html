Emerging Markets
Analysis

An 18-week journey across ASEAN, South Asia, and the Middle East — what makes these markets matter to Taiwan, and what that means for the people working in them.

Tuesdays · 09:00–12:00 18 weeks Groups of 3 Asst. Prof. Alexis Goh · MA309

What this course is about

Taiwan doesn't exist in isolation

Taiwan depends on trade routes through the Middle East, manufacturing partnerships across ASEAN, energy suppliers in the Gulf, and an increasingly contested position in global supply chains. This course builds working knowledge of the markets that matter most to Taiwan — and asks you to think critically about what those markets mean for business.

From Week 4 onward, your group takes the lead on presenting one country to the class. The instructor then uses the remaining two hours for discussion, debate, and analysis. You're not here to sit and listen — you're here to think, present, and engage.

Not all countries covered are formally classified as "emerging markets." They've been selected for their strategic relevance to Taiwan's economy and business interests.

  • 01Know the geography, demographics, politics, and economy of major markets across ASEAN, South Asia, and the Middle East.
  • 02Understand the "emerging market" label — and where it breaks down.
  • 03Compare each market to Taiwan across economy, risk, and trade.
  • 04Build and defend a reasoned business judgment.
  • 05Use AI tools critically — as a research aid, not a substitute for thinking.

Good to know

How this course works

🎤

You present, the instructor facilitates

From Week 4, each group owns the first 40 minutes of class. The instructor uses the remaining 2 hours for discussion, activities, and deeper analysis.

📊

Use the instructor's slides as your base

Slide decks go up on TronClass before class. Update the data, keep what's relevant, and add slides on anything you find interesting — within your 30-minute limit.

🤖

AI is allowed — think first

AI tools are permitted for research and writing throughout this course. The position and reasoning must always be your own.

✍️

English is the language of instruction

This is an EMI course. All assessments are expected in English. Chinese responses in the mid-semester task are accepted but carry a score ceiling.

🌏

Taiwan is always the reference point

Every country is compared to Taiwan — size, wages, cost of living, credit ratings, trade. You'll leave knowing where Taiwan sits in the world.

⏰

Punctuality matters

There are no weekly quizzes to enforce attendance. Punctuality and engagement count toward the 5% Lecturer Discretion component. Show up on time.
