About This Course

Think like a
digital entrepreneur.

This course teaches you the decisions that determine whether an eCommerce venture succeeds or fails — who to sell to, what to say, how to reach them, and how to design an experience people trust.

🇹🇼
Taiwan-First
Real Taiwanese companies, platforms, and consumer behaviors at the core of every lesson.
🏪
Real Client Brief
Design a marketing plan for a real Taiwanese entrepreneur — with a cash prize for the winning group.
💡
No Coding Required
This is a strategy and business course. No programming skills needed — just sharp thinking.
🌐
Live Store Project
Build and present a real, publicly accessible online store as your final project.

On AI Tools

You are encouraged to use AI tools — including Google Gemini — throughout this course. However, every assessment is designed so that generic AI-generated answers cannot substitute for real strategic thinking about specific Taiwanese markets, companies, and customers. AI is your thinking partner, not your ghostwriter.

Individual · Unannounced Weekly Quizzes — 30% (6 × 5%)
▾

Six short quizzes administered at the instructor's discretion throughout the semester, without prior notice. Any class session may begin with a quiz. Be prepared every week.

  • Format: 3 multiple-choice questions + 2 fill-in-the-blanks
  • Duration: 15 minutes at the start of class
  • Coverage: material up to and including the previous class
  • Quizzes are unannounced — specific quiz dates are not disclosed in advance
  • No make-up quizzes — absent students score zero for that quiz
Individual · Week 9 Mid-Term Exam — 20%
▾

A 24-hour take-home exam distributed at the end of Week 9's class. Analytical in nature — no multiple choice.

  • Format: 2 mini case studies requiring analytical responses
  • Covers Weeks 1–8
  • Collaboration with classmates is permitted
  • Written responses must be your own — identical answers between students will be treated as an integrity issue
  • Submitted via TRONCLASS within 24 hours of distribution
Groups · Week 14 GA1 — Marketing Channel Plan — 20%
▾

Design a 2-channel marketing plan for a real Taiwanese food entrepreneur's product. The client will be present on presentation day to evaluate your work and ask questions.

  • Groups of 2–3 students
  • Class is split into two product groups: Frozen Taiwanese Prawns or 一口烏魚子 (Bite-sized Mullet Roe)
  • All teams within each group work from the same real client brief
  • Deliverables: detailed 2-channel plan + a professional pitch memo addressed to the client
  • Budget constraint: NT$3,000/month maximum for paid marketing
🏆 Cash prize of at least NT$1,000 for the winning group
Pairs · Weeks 17–18 Dream Store Final Project — 30%
▾

The centrepiece of the course. With a partner, you will design a Dream Online Store — a real, publicly accessible storefront built on Google Sites, backed by a Business & Tech Roadmap document explaining every strategic decision you made.

  • The Store (35%): 6 required pages — homepage, product catalogue, individual product page, brand story, promotions, and contact
  • The Roadmap (65%): Strategy · Design Rationale · Marketing Plan · The Black Box (payment, logistics, inventory systems)
  • Graded across four pillars: Strategy 40 pts · Design 20 pts · Marketing 30 pts · Black Box 10 pts
  • Presented live in Weeks 17–18 with instructor Q&A
  • No coding required — store is built using Google Sites and embedded tools

How the Course Works

Every week is a
3-hour session.

Structured as three 50-minute blocks with 10-minute breaks. Lecture and activity every week — never lecture-only.

🎯
Session 1 — Lecture
Core concept introduced with Taiwan-specific examples, real brand cases, and live website analysis.
⚡
Session 2 — Activity
Apply what you just learned immediately — group exercises, peer analysis, design workshops, or critiques.
💬
Session 3 — Discussion or Build
Debate real eCommerce decisions, present findings, or work directly on your Dream Store project.
📱
Mobile-First Thinking
Bring your phone to every class. Real-world testing on actual devices is a core part of Weeks 13–17.

What You Need

✓ A Google account (for Google Sites, Docs, Forms & Gemini)
✓ Access to TRONCLASS for submissions and course materials
✓ A smartphone for mobile UX testing in Weeks 13–17
✓ No textbook required — all readings under 15 pages/week

Who This Course Is For

Year 3 BIM students who want to understand how digital businesses actually work — not just how to use the tools, but why certain strategies succeed and others fail. If you have ever wondered how to turn a product idea into a real online business, this course gives you the framework to do it.
